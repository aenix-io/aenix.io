## Quick facts

- **What it is:** an interactive calculator for VMware/VCF cost versus an open-platform alternative.
- **Who it's for:** infrastructure, finance and procurement teams scoping a VMware exit.
- **Inputs:** CPU cores, VMware cost per core/year, Aenix support per core/year, one-time migration cost.
- **Typical outcome:** 30–60% cost reduction on workloads that move (your numbers may differ).
- **License:** the target platform is [Cozystack](https://cozystack.io), Apache 2.0 — no per-core licensing.
- **Common pitfall:** comparing license-to-license only, ignoring migration cost and the workloads that should stay put.

## How the calculation works

- **VMware cost per year** = cores × VMware cost per core/year.
- **Aenix support per year** = cores × Aenix support per core/year (the platform itself is Apache 2.0).
- **Net saving per year** = VMware annual − Aenix annual.
- **3-year net saving** = net annual × 3 − one-time migration cost.
- **Payback** = migration cost ÷ monthly net saving.

These are deliberately simple inputs so the output is defensible. A full TCO includes power, hardware refresh, staff and the workloads you keep in the cloud — we model those with you on a call.

## FAQ

**Is this an official VMware/Broadcom calculator?**
No. It's an independent estimator built by Aenix to compare VMware/VCF spend against an open-platform alternative. Use your own renewal figures.

**What cost per core should I enter?**
Use your current VMware/VCF subscription cost divided by licensed cores. If you only have a total renewal number, divide it by your core count.

**Does the platform really have no license cost?**
The platform is Cozystack, Apache 2.0 — no per-core/per-socket license. You pay for support and/or the build engagement, both editable above.

**Do I have to migrate everything?**
No. Savings apply to the workloads that move; some workloads should stay where they are. The [VMware migration](/migration/vmware/) page covers sequencing.

**Can you validate my numbers?**
Yes — a discovery call produces an honest, workload-level TCO, including what to keep in the cloud.
