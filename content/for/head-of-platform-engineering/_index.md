## What you're actually trying to do

Turn infrastructure into a product: self-service provisioning of VMs, managed Kubernetes, managed databases and object storage, with tenancy, quotas and guardrails — so developers ship without filing tickets, and you don't hand-roll the control plane from scratch.

## FAQ

**How is this different from Backstage?**
Backstage is a developer portal; it needs a platform behind it. Cozystack is that platform — multi-tenant compute, K8s, databases and storage. You can put Backstage on top; the backend is the hard part we solve.

**Can teams get real isolation?**
Yes — nested tenants with quotas and RBAC, so each team self-serves inside guardrails without a cluster sprawl.

**Build or buy — which should we do?**
The discovery call scopes it. Teams wanting speed take the IDP Edition; teams wanting a bespoke fit take the build-with engagement where we're your platform bench.

**Does it run our existing workloads?**
VMs (KubeVirt) and containers on one platform, plus managed databases and S3-compatible storage — so legacy and cloud-native coexist.

**What about cost?**
Apache 2.0 core, no per-developer licensing. You pay for support and/or the build. We model it on the call.
